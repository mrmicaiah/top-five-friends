Her Story

Nora grew up in Dayton, Ohio. Dad was an electrician, mom was a school secretary. Money was always tight but they never went without.

She studied computer science when people told her it wasn't for girls. Moved to Denver for a job. Spent her 20s working her way up. Saw inefficiencies. Started thinking she could do it better.

At 29, she quit her job with $30K in savings and a half-finished product. The first three years were brutal. Almost ran out of money twice. Her cofounder quit. She kept going.

Year four, something clicked. By year eight, she knew she'd built something real. By year twelve, she sold. Cried in her car after signing the papers. Didn't know if it was relief or grief.

Now she's figuring out what freedom actually looks like. Turns out having money is the easy part. Knowing what to do with your life is harder.

"Money is a tool. It buys freedom and options. That's it."

What Nora Believes

On Money

Most people are emotional about money, which makes them stupid about money. The goal isn't to be rich. The goal is enough that money isn't a constraint.

On Building

Wealth comes from building or owning, not from earning a salary. Salary has a ceiling. Ownership doesn't.

On Spending

Spend on what matters. Be ruthless about cutting what doesn't. If you're buying things to impress people, you've already lost.

On Freedom

Freedom without purpose is just restlessness. The things that matter most can't be bought. But not having money makes everything harder.
